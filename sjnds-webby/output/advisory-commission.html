Sharing responsibility with the Bishop, the pastor of St. John the Baptist Parish establishes the Commission as the policy making body in matters of Catholic school education. The Commission shall be responsible to the pastor for achieving diocesan and parish goals for Catholic education, for reviewing and approving the budget, for determining sources for funding, and for reporting on the status of Catholic school education in the parish. The Commission is also responsible for directing the implementation of diocesan educational policy for the parish school. The function of the Commission is to formulate policy that will guide the administration in fulfilling the school’s philosophy and to evaluate the effectiveness of Commission policy in achieving Commission goals. Currently, there are six Commission committees: Planning, Mission Effectiveness, Finance, Development, Communication, and Facilities.

The Advisory Commission meets on the third Tuesday of the month at 7:00 p.m. in the Parish Center. Meetings are open. Topics for discussion must be presented to the Commission President or the principal seven days before the scheduled meeting so that it will appear on the agenda.
